Science Topics · 지문 1 · 문장삽입
Does the Future Exist?
미래는 존재하는가 — 생물학적 비유로 본 시간의 예측 가능성
문장삽입 (Sentence Insertion)
주어진 문장이 들어가기에 가장 적절한 곳은?
In much the same way, some scholars argue, the future somehow "grows" out of conditions in the past and the present.
There is considerable debate about whether or not the future really exists and, if it does, where one could find it. ( ① ) Let us leave aside the realm of science fiction, where time travelers are able actually to experience the future "before it happens," and concentrate on those visions in which the future is somehow knowable because it is connected to the past and the present. ( ② ) Some scholars see time in much the same way as biologists see living organisms. ( ③ ) If biologists know the genetic material of an organism and the conditions in which it lives (its nutrition, for example), and they know the laws that govern its development, they can predict how the mature organism will look and behave. ( ④ ) There is / a direct and almost physical connection / between past and future. ( ⑤ ) If one perceives the present and the past correctly, and if one also knows the laws that govern development or growth, then one can forecast fairly accurately what the future will be like.
01 문장삽입 정답 & 풀이
📌 주어진 문장
In much the same way, some scholars argue, the future somehow "grows" out of conditions in the past and the present.
같은 방식으로, 일부 학자들은 미래가 과거와 현재의 조건에서 어떻게든 "자라난다"고 주장한다.
정답 & 핵심 단서
정답 ④
핵심 단서 — "In much the same way"(똑같은 방식으로): 이 연결어는 앞에 비교 대상이 먼저 나와야 함을 의미. 즉 "어떤 방식"이 앞 문장에 제시되어야 함.

논리 흐름:
• ③ 앞: "Some scholars see time … as biologists see living organisms" (학자들이 시간을 생물학자가 유기체 보듯 봄 — 비유 도입)
• ③ 뒤: "If biologists know the genetic material … they can predict how the mature organism will look" (생물학자는 유전 물질·법칙을 알면 성숙한 유기체를 예측)
• ④ 정답 위치: 생물학자의 예측 설명 직후 → "In much the same way … the future grows out of past and present" (같은 방식으로 미래도 과거·현재에서 자라남)
• ④ 뒤: "There is a direct and almost physical connection between past and future" (과거와 미래의 직접적 연결 — 주어진 문장의 결론을 이어받음)

오답 배제: ②·③에 넣으면 아직 "biologists가 예측한다"는 비유가 완성되지 않아 "In much the same way"의 비교 대상이 없음. ⑤에 넣으면 "There is a direct connection"(④뒤)이 주어진 문장보다 먼저 와 논리가 끊김.
02 단락 구조 독해 (삽입 후 완성된 글)
📘 INTRO — 미래 존재 논쟁 & 범위 설정
There is considerable debate about whether or not the future really exists … Let us leave aside the realm of science fiction … and concentrate on those visions in which the future is somehow knowable because it is connected to the past and the present.
Summary
The author sets aside science fiction and focuses on views in which the future is knowable through its connection to past and present.
→ SF식 시간여행은 배제 / 미래가 과거·현재와 연결되어 "알 수 있다"는 관점에 집중
📗 DEV — 생물학적 비유
Some scholars see time in much the same way as biologists see living organisms. If biologists know the genetic material of an organism and the conditions in which it lives … they can predict how the mature organism will look and behave. [④ 주어진 문장] In much the same way … the future "grows" out of conditions in the past and the present.
Summary
Just as biologists predict an organism's development from its genes and laws, scholars argue the future grows out of past and present conditions.
→ 생물학자: 유전물질+법칙 → 성숙체 예측 / 같은 방식으로 미래도 과거·현재 조건에서 자라남 (핵심 비유)
📙 CONCL — 결론: 예측 가능성
There is / a direct and almost physical connection / between past and future. If one perceives the present and the past correctly, and if one also knows the laws that govern development or growth, then one can forecast fairly accurately what the future will be like.
Summary
A direct connection links past and future; correctly grasping past/present plus the laws of growth allows fairly accurate forecasting.
→ 과거-미래의 직접적 연결 / 과거·현재를 정확히 인식 + 성장 법칙을 알면 미래를 꽤 정확히 예측
03 구문 해석
주어(S)동사(V)/ 의미 단위 구분나머지는 목적어·보어·수식어
There is / considerable debate / about whether or not the future really exists and, if it does, where one could find it.
미래가 정말 존재하는지, 그리고 존재한다면 그것을 어디서 찾을 수 있는지에 대해 상당한 논쟁이 있다. ★ whether or not: ~인지 아닌지(명사절) / if it does = if the future exists / where one could find it: 간접의문문

시간 여행자가 미래를 "일어나기 전에" 실제로 경험할 수 있는 공상과학의 영역은 제쳐두고, 미래가 과거 및 현재와 연결되어 있기 때문에 어떻게든 알 수 있는 그런 관점에 집중하자. ★ Let us leave aside ~ and concentrate on ~: 명령문 병렬 / where: 관계부사(science fiction 수식) / in which: 전치사+관계대명사(visions 수식)

일부 학자들은 생물학자가 살아있는 유기체를 보는 것과 거의 같은 방식으로 시간을 본다. ★ in much the same way as: ~와 거의 같은 방식으로 / as: 양태 접속사

만약 생물학자가 유기체의 유전 물질과 그것이 사는 조건(예를 들어 영양 상태)을 알고, 그 발달을 지배하는 법칙을 안다면, 성숙한 유기체가 어떻게 보이고 행동할지 예측할 수 있다. ★ in which it lives: 전치사+관계대명사(conditions 수식) / that govern: 관계절(laws 수식) / how the mature organism will look: 간접의문문(predict의 목적어)

같은 방식으로, 일부 학자들은 미래가 과거와 현재의 조건에서 어떻게든 "자라난다"고 주장한다. ★ In much the same way: 앞 비유와 동일 방식 / some scholars argue: 삽입절 / grow out of: ~에서 자라나다·생겨나다

과거와 미래 사이에는 직접적이고 거의 물리적인 연결이 있다. ★ connection between A and B: A와 B 사이의 연결 / almost physical: 거의 물리적인(강조)

만약 현재와 과거를 정확히 인식하고, 발달이나 성장을 지배하는 법칙도 안다면, 미래가 어떨지 꽤 정확하게 예측할 수 있다. ★ if ~ and if ~: 조건절 병렬 / that govern: 관계절(laws 수식) / what the future will be like: 간접의문문(forecast의 목적어) / fairly accurately: 꽤 정확하게

Science Topics · 지문 2 · 주제 완성
Sensory-Specific Satiety
감각 특정 포만감 — 음식의 다양성이 과식을 부른다
주제 완성 (Topic Completion)
다음 글의 주제를 완성하시오.
Sensory-specific satiety is defined as a decrease in appetite, or the subjective liking for the food that is consumed, with little change in the hedonics of uneaten food. As a result of sensory-specific satiety, when people consume a variety of foods, they tend to overeat. A greater variety of food leads people to eat more than they would otherwise. So, being full and feeling sated are separate matters. The recovery of appetite or the motivation to eat is apparent to anyone who has consumed a large meal and is quite full, and does not require additional energy or nutrients to meet their daily needs, but decides to consume additional calories after seeing the dessert cart. Small changes in the sensory properties of foods are sufficient to increase food intake. For example, subjects who were presented with different shapes of pasta showed increased hedonic ratings and increased energy consumption relative to subjects eating only a single shape of pasta.
* satiety: 포만(감)    ** hedonics: 쾌락    *** sated: 충분히 만족한
01 단락 구조 독해
📘 INTRO — 개념 정의
Sensory-specific satiety is defined as a decrease in appetite, or the subjective liking for the food that is consumed, with little change in the hedonics of uneaten food.
Summary
Sensory-specific satiety is the drop in appetite for a food being eaten, while the appeal of uneaten foods remains largely unchanged.
→ 감각 특정 포만감 = 먹고 있는 음식에 대한 식욕은 감소하지만, 먹지 않은 음식의 매력(쾌락)은 거의 변하지 않음
📗 DEV — 결과: 과식 유발
As a result of sensory-specific satiety, when people consume a variety of foods, they tend to overeat. A greater variety of food leads people to eat more than they would otherwise. So, being full and feeling sated are separate matters.
Summary
Because of this effect, dietary variety drives overeating — being physically full and feeling satisfied are distinct.
→ 다양한 음식 → 과식 경향 / "배부름(full)"과 "만족감(sated)"은 별개 / 다양성↑ → 섭취량↑
📙 CONCL — 사례 & 증거
The recovery of appetite … is apparent to anyone who has consumed a large meal and is quite full … but decides to consume additional calories after seeing the dessert cart. Small changes in the sensory properties of foods are sufficient to increase food intake. For example, subjects … presented with different shapes of pasta showed increased hedonic ratings and increased energy consumption relative to subjects eating only a single shape of pasta.
Summary
Appetite revives at the sight of dessert; even minor sensory changes (e.g. different pasta shapes) raise enjoyment and intake.
→ 디저트 카트를 보면 식욕 회복 / 작은 감각 변화(파스타 모양)만으로도 쾌락 평가·섭취 에너지 증가 (실험 증거)

Science Topics · 지문 3 · 문장삽입
How Bacteria Navigate
박테리아의 화학물질 탐지 — 시간으로 공간을 다루는 영리한 방법
문장삽입 (Sentence Insertion)
주어진 문장이 들어가기에 가장 적절한 곳은?
The bacterium will swim in a straight line as long as the chemicals it senses seem better now than those it sensed a moment ago.
A bacterium is so small that its sensors alone can give it no indication of the direction that a good or bad chemical is coming from. ( ① ) To overcome this problem, the bacterium uses time to help it deal with space. ( ② ) The bacterium is not interested in how much of a chemical is present at any given moment, but rather in whether that concentration is increasing or decreasing. ( ③ ) After all, if the bacterium swam in a straight line simply because the concentration of a desirable chemical was high, it might travel away from chemical nirvana, not toward it, depending on the direction it's pointing. ( ④ ) The bacterium solves this problem in an ingenious manner: as it senses its world, one mechanism registers what conditions are like right now, and another records how things were a few moments ago. When trying to establish what is meant by digital preservation, the first question that must be addressed is: what are you actually trying to preserve? This is clear in the analog environment where the information content is inextricably fixed to the physical medium. In the digital environment, the medium is not part of the  . Self-Other Discrimination in Robots
로봇의 자기-타자 구별 — 목표 지향 행동의 전제 조건
문장삽입 (Sentence Insertion)
주어진 글이 들어가기에 가장 적절한 곳은?
If not, the robot might endlessly chase itself rather than the blocks.
People involved in the conception and engineering of robots designed to perceive and act know how fundamental is the ability to discriminate oneself from other entities in the environment. Without such an ability, no goal-oriented action would be possible. ( ① ) Imagine that you have to build a robot able to search for blocks scattered in a room in order to pile them. ( ② ) Even this simple task would require that your machine be able to discriminate between stimulation that originates from its own machinery and stimulation that originates from the blocks in the environment. ( ③ ) Suppose that you equip your robot with an artificial eye and an artificial arm to detect, grab, and pile the blocks. ( ④ ) To be successful, your machine will have to have some built-in system enabling it to discriminate between the detection of a block and the detection of its own arm. ( ⑤ ) Your robot would engage in circular, self-centered acts that would drive it away from the target or external goal.
* entity: 실재물(物)
01 문장삽입 정답 & 풀이
📌 주어진 문장
If not, the robot might endlessly chase itself rather than the blocks.
그렇지 않으면, 로봇은 블록 대신 끝없이 자기 자신을 쫓을 수도 있다.
정답 & 핵심 단서
정답 ⑤
핵심 단서 — "If not"(그렇지 않으면): 이 조건은 앞에 긍정 조건(구별 시스템을 갖춘 경우)이 먼저 나와야 함. "If not"은 그 긍정 조건의 부정(시스템이 없으면)을 의미.

논리 흐름:
• ⑤ 앞: "your machine will have to have some built-in system enabling it to discriminate between the detection of a block and the detection of its own arm" (블록 감지와 자기 팔 감지를 구별하는 내장 시스템이 필요 — 긍정 조건)
• ⑤ 정답 위치: "If not, the robot might endlessly chase itself" (그렇지 않으면 = 구별 시스템이 없으면, 로봇이 자기를 쫓음)
• ⑤ 뒤: "Your robot would engage in circular, self-centered acts that would drive it away from the target" (로봇이 순환적·자기중심적 행동 → 목표에서 멀어짐 — 주어진 문장의 "자기를 쫓는" 결과를 부연)

오답 배제: ⑤ 외 위치엔 "If not"이 받을 긍정 조건(구별 시스템 필요)이 직전에 없어 논리가 성립하지 않음. 주어진 문장과 그 뒤 "circular, self-centered acts"가 의미상 연결(자기를 쫓음 = 순환적 자기중심 행동).
02 단락 구조 독해 (삽입 후 완성된 글)
📘 INTRO — 자기-타자 구별의 근본성
People involved in the conception and engineering of robots designed to perceive and act know how fundamental is the ability to discriminate oneself from other entities … Without such an ability, no goal-oriented action would be possible.
Summary
Robotics engineers know that self-other discrimination is fundamental — without it, no goal-oriented action is possible.
→ 로봇 설계자들은 자기-타자 구별 능력이 근본적임을 안다 / 이 능력 없이는 목표 지향 행동 불가능
📗 DEV — 예시: 블록 쌓기 로봇

→ 블록 쌓기 같은 단순 작업도 자기 기계에서 온 자극 vs 블록에서 온 자극 구별 필요 / 인공 눈·팔 장착
📙 CONCL — 결론: 구별 시스템의 필수성

[⑤ 주어진 문장] If not, the robot might endlessly chase itself rather than the blocks. Your robot would engage in circular, self-centered acts that would drive it away from the target or external goal.

→ 블록 감지 vs 자기 팔 감지를 구별하는 내장 시스템 필수 / 없으면 자기를 쫓는 순환적·자기중심 행동 → 목표에서 이탈
