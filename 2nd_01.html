Benefits of Simplifying Observer's Job
관찰자 평가 작업을 단순화하는 것의 이점 · 빈칸 추론

To illustrate the benefits of                                the observer's job, consider psychologist William Ickes's research on "everyday mind reading."

One way he studies such mind reading is by having two strangers interact.

He tapes the interaction and then has each participant view the tape.

Participants are to stop the tape at different points and say what they are thinking.

Then, participants see the tape again and are to stop it at different points and write down what their interaction partner was thinking at that point.

Observers rate the degree to which the participant's guess about what the partner was thinking matches what the partner was actually thinking.

Ickes could have had observers make their judgments on a 0 (not at all) to 100 (completely) scale.

However, he had observers use a 3-point scale with "0" being "essentially different content," "1" being "similar, but not the same, content," and "2" being "essentially the same content."

By using fewer categories, raters found the job of rating easier and were able to make reliable ratings.

Difficulty of Defining Instinct
'본능'을 정의하기 어려운 이유 · 빈칸 추론

Instinct is the most difficult of the motivation words to define. At the everyday level it is used often.

For example, a sports commentator might say, 'He made that pass instinctively.'

This means that the pass was made easily, automatically, with great skill and with a seemingly intuitive knowledge of the state of the game.

However, at a technical level the word refers to behaviours that are built in,                               , and are specific to a species.

So, a spider builds a web instinctively and a bird's mating display might be instinctive.

It is unlikely, though, that there is any equivalent instinctive behaviour in human beings.

Some people might argue that a mother's reaction to her newly born child is instinctive, but certainly not all mothers react in the same way and even when they do, they express it in myriad forms.

In general, instinct has been found to be a not very useful construct in giving accounts of the 'why' of behaviour.

💡 일타강사 팁: 일상생활에서 쓰는 '본능적(instinctive)'이라는 말과, 생물학/전문적(technical level)으로 쓰는 '본능'의 정의가 다름을 설명하는 글입니다.

전문적 수준에서 동물의 본능은 선천적(built in)이고 종에 특유(specific to a species)해야 합니다. 빈칸에 들어갈 특징을 찾으려면 뒷문장 인간의 예시를 보면 됩니다. "인간 어머니의 모성애를 본능이라 주장할지 모르나, 모든 어머니가 똑같은 방식(in the same way)으로 반응하지 않으며 무수히 다양한 형태(myriad forms)로 표현한다."

False Memory by TV Coverage
TV 보도로 인한 기억의 왜곡과 거짓 기억 · 빈칸 추론

An indication of the power of TV to "capture" people's memory is provided by the results of a study by James Ost and coworkers, who approached people in an English shopping center and asked if they would be willing to participate in a study examining how well people can remember tragic events.

The target event involved Princess Diana and her companion Dodi Fayed, whose deaths in a car crash in Paris on August 31, 1997, were widely covered on British television.

Participants were asked to respond to the following statement: "Have you seen the paparazzi's video-recording of the car crash in which Diana, Princess of Wales, and Dodi Fayed lost their lives?"

Of the 45 people who responded to this question, 20 said they had seen the film.

This was, however, impossible, because no such film exists. The car crash was reported on TV, but not actually shown.

                               apparently caused some people to remember something - seeing the film - that didn't actually occur.

정답: ④ The extensive media coverage of this event
💡 일타강사 팁: TV 방송이 사람들의 기억을 '사로잡아(capture)' 왜곡시킬 수 있다는 연구 결과입니다.

다이애나 비의 자동차 사고 당시, 뉴스에서 그 사고를 대대적으로 보도하긴 했지만 실제 충돌 영상(비디오)은 존재하지 않았습니다. 그럼에도 45명 중 20명이나 '그 비디오를 본 적 있다'고 대답했죠(거짓 기억).

존재하지도 않는 영상을 보았다고 착각하게 만든 원인은 무엇일까요? 바로 지문 앞부분에 나온 "were widely covered on British television (영국 TV에서 널리 보도되었다)" 때문입니다. TV에서 지겹도록 들은 이야기를 영상으로 본 것처럼 뇌가 착각한 것이죠. 따라서 '이 사건에 대한 광범위한 미디어 보도'를 뜻하는 ④번이 정답입니다.

🟢 핵심 구문 & 문법 포인트

① 소유격 관계대명사 whose: "...and her companion Dodi Fayed, [whose deaths... were widely covered]". 선행사인 Dodi Fayed (및 다이애나)의 '죽음(deaths)'이라는 소유 관계를 나타내므로 whose가 쓰였습니다. (and their deaths로 바꿀 수 있음)

② 전치사 + 관계대명사 (in which): "the car crash [in which Diana... lost their lives]". 선행사가 car crash이므로 in the car crash에서 in이 앞으로 이동한 것입니다. 뒤에 주어와 동사(목적어)를 갖춘 완전한 절이 옵니다.

③ 간접의문문과 접속사 if: "asked if they would be willing to~"에서 if는 만약~라면이 아니라 '인지 아닌지(=whether)'를 뜻하는 명사절 접속사입니다. (ask의 목적어)

03 Topic & 04 Logical Framework
TV coverage can "capture" people's memory and lead to false memories of details that never happened.
(TV 보도는 사람들의 기억을 '사로잡아' 결코 일어나지 않은 세부사항에 대한 거짓 기억을 유발할 수 있다.)
StepLogicMy Summary
① Intro Study context: Ost and colleagues recruited shoppers in England for a study about memory for tragic events. 영국에서 비극적 사건에 대한 기억 연구를 위해 참가자들을 모집함.
② Dev 1 Target Event: Diana and Dodi's 1997 crash. Key Question: "Did you see a paparazzi video of the crash?" Key Result: 20 of 45 people said they had seen the video. TV에 많이 보도된 다이애나비 사고 영상(실제론 없음)을 봤냐는 질문에 절반 가까이가 봤다고 답함.
③ Dev 2 Reality check: No such crash video exists; TV reported the crash but did not show it. 사실 확인: TV가 보도는 했지만 그런 영상은 존재하지 않음.
④ Conclusion Reasoning: People remember "seeing the film" even though it did not occur due to media coverage. 광범위한 미디어 보도가 사람들에게 영상을 본 것 같은 '거짓 기억'을 심어줌.
