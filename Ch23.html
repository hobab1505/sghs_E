Chaos Theory & the Magnetic Pendulum
혼돈 이론과 자기 진자 — 반복, 비선형 시스템, 분기점

본문 — 원문 (글의 순서 문제)

A good example of chaos is the magnetic pendulum진자, 추(錘) sold as an executive toy사무실 책상용 소품. It has four magnets arranged in a square at the base and a pendulum that swings back and forth between them.

(A)

In order to produce chaos, the iteration반복 has to be within what is called a nonlinear비선형의 system. Nor are all nonlinear systems chaotic: to become so they need to be pushed beyond a certain point, called a bifurcation분기(分岐). Before that point is reached they may behave in a quite orderly질서 있는 fashion.

(B)

Release the pendulum and note the magnets that it visits, and in what order. If the pendulum is released from the same position a second time, the pattern of movement may at first be the same but soon it will become completely different. In fact, the pattern of its movement is chaotic.

(C)

No matter how much care is taken to start the pendulum in the same position, it will visit an entirely different set of points on the two occasions. Chaotic systems are generated by iteration, though not all iteration leads to chaos.

Relative Velocity & Momentum
상대 속도와 운동량 — 프랑스 조종사의 총알 잡기
주어진 단어로 제목 완성
본문 — 원문

There is one important thing to note about momentum운동량. Since motion is relative, only relative velocities속도들 are relevant. In fact, when you think about it, all velocities are relative. Thus, on the surface of the earth, we always reckon측정하다, 생각하다 velocity with respect to the ground — the velocity of the earth itself does not matter. There is a true story of a French pilot in World War II who stretched his hand out of his small airplane window and caught a bullet. How is this possible? The velocity of the bullet must have been about the same as the velocity of his plane. This can happen, because a bullet slows down to such a velocity toward the end of its flight path due to the action of air drag공기 저항. Since only relative velocity matters, the momentum of the bullet was negligible무시할 수 있는 as far as the pilot was concerned. Thus, he would have had little problem in catching the bullet.

본문 — 원문 (밑줄 ①~⑤)

Hipparchus of Nicaea was the most important astronomer of the Hellenistic Age after Aristarchus of Samos. Unlike Aristarchus, Hipparchus believed in the geocentric지구 중심적인 universe of Aristotle. A noted mathematician, Hipparchus gauged측정하다 the movement of the sun and the moon ① using an astronomical model devised by Apollonius of Perga. Hipparchus worked at Alexandria and Rhodes; at the latter city, he created a kind of astronomical research center ② in which he cataloged목록화하다 the stars according to brightness. He produced a star chart of about 850 stars, ③ tracking their movements and relative brightness. Hipparchus made more accurate measurements than Aristarchus ④ was of the distance of the moon and sun from the earth. To account for설명하다 the orbit of the sun around the earth he developed the idea of the eccentric이심원, ⑤ which Ptolemy of Alexandria would expand upon.
