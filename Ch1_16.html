다음 글의 밑줄 친 부분 중, 어법상 틀린 것은?
The current trend of increasing global atmospheric temperatures and increasing seawater acidity reduces the overall capacity of oceans ① to absorb more CO2. By the end of the century, current levels of carbon dioxide emissions could result in the most acidic levels of ocean pH in 20 million years, ⑤ which would have severe adverse effects on ocean water chemistry (both coastal and deep sea), the marine life and food webs, and the function of oceans as a carbon reservoir.

01 어법 오류 분석 — 밑줄 ①②③④⑤ 전체 검토
번호문제의 표현판정근거
①to absorb✅ 정상capacity to absorb: capacity 수식 형용사적 to부정사 — "흡수하는 능력"의 의미로 적절

③eroding❌ 오류주어 "complex relationships … in marine systems"에 대한 서술어 동사가 필요. eroding은 현재분사 → erode(동사원형)로 고쳐야 함. 뒤에 "and threaten"과 병렬을 이루어야 함.

정답 & 핵심 어법 포인트
③ eroding → erode
오류 분석: 문장 구조: "complex relationships between water temperatures and ocean acidity in marine systems eroding calcification rates … and threaten the survival of coral reefs." 주어(complex relationships)에 대한 본동사가 없음. "eroding"을 현재분사로 쓰면 서술어 역할을 하지 못함 → erode로 수정해야 "erode … and threaten"의 병렬 동사 구조가 완성됨.

핵심 판별법: 문장에 동사(정동사)가 있는지 먼저 확인. -ing 형태가 보이면 ① 현재분사(수식) ② 동명사(주어/목적어) ③ 진행시제(be + -ing) 중 어느 것인지 판단 후 본동사 존재 여부 체크.

How Large Language Models Work
대형 언어 모델(LLM)의 학습 원리와 구조
주제 (Topic) · 정답 ①
다음 글의 주제로 가장 적절한 것은?
Large Language Models (LLMs) are extremely strong artificial intelligence (AI) systems that use sophisticated algorithms and enormous volumes of data to understand, interpret, and generate human language. Deep learning techniques, specifically neural networks, are typically used in the construction of LLMs in order to process and learn from massive volumes of data. LLMs must be fundamentally trained on a large volume of data, typically measured in petabytes. Large strings of numbers, referred to as "weights" or "parameters," combined with code that decodes and applies the numbers make up machine learning models. The data that models learn from is not stored in or contained in the models themselves. Rather, certain numbers within a model adjust slightly in response to new information as it gains knowledge. Large language models require the successful development of several essential steps, which are involved in the training process. Large-scale text data collection and preprocessing from a variety of sources, including books, articles, websites, and other textual corpora, usually marks the start of the process.

By the 1950s, social theorists began thinking and writing about age, aging, generations, and the life course. Their work remains relevant today. The first serious attempt to look at the social importance of age groups ① was made by the German sociologist Karl Mannheim in an essay titled "The Problem of Generations," which was first published in 1927. Mannheim defined generation as a category of people ② born within a specific historical era or time period. For Mannheim, a generation was also characterized by common world views that distinguished ③ it from other generations. Mannheim was keenly aware that accident of birth timing did not automatically create these common understandings and worldviews; he observed that social and social psychological processes led some members of a generation ④ develop an identity and consciousness with their age peers. Mannheim suggested that generational consciousness arose not from merely being born at the same time but from being exposed to the same kinds of experiences and historical events in a common social and political environment. According to Mannheim, ⑤ belonging to a generation is a combination of a state of mind and an age grouping.
