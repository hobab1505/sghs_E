Volunteer cartographers using GPS and online tools are transforming digital mapmaking by crowdsourcing local geographic data — democratizing a field once dominated by professionals — while also raising new questions about data reliability and political control of geographic information.

Reading Comprehension — 5문항 모범 답안
Question 1
What motivated Richard Hintz to contribute to Google Maps?
모범 답안 (영어):
Richard Hintz was motivated partly by self-interest — he wants to know where he is going — and partly by the satisfaction of helping others. He described his contributions as acts of "geo-volunteerism," noting that while his primary reason was personal convenience, knowing that his edits also helped other users was an added attraction.

한국어 해설:
힌츠의 동기는 두 가지이다. ① 자기 이익(self-interest) — 자신이 어디로 가는지 알기 위해 ② 타인에게도 도움이 된다는 부가적 매력. "geo-volunteerism"이라는 표현이 핵심 — 개인적 이유와 공공적 기여가 함께함.
Question 2
In what way are volunteer cartographers changing the field of mapmaking?
모범 답안 (영어):
Volunteer cartographers are democratizing mapmaking — a field that was once the exclusive domain of professionals and specialists. Using GPS devices and easy-to-use software, they are creating digital maps where none existed, fixing errors, and editing the underlying maps of streets, highways, rivers, and coastlines. Like Wikipedia contributors, they are opening up a previously closed, expert-controlled field to ordinary citizens worldwide.

한국어 해설:
핵심 개념: 민주화(democratization). 전문가·전문 기업의 독점 영역이었던 지도 제작을 일반 시민에게 개방. ① 기존에 없던 지도 창조 ② 오류 수정 ③ 도로·강·해안선 등 기반 지도 직접 편집 — 이전의 단순 정보 추가(범죄 통계, 임대 정보)에서 한 단계 진화.
Question 3
Why does Google value user contributions to online maps?
모범 답안 (영어):
Google values user contributions because maps play a growing strategic role in its business, especially as people increasingly use cellphones to find businesses, restaurants, and other destinations. Google needs reliable, precise location data to serve these users effectively. As John Hanke stated, "The way you get that data is having users precisely locate things." User-generated data also allows Google to bypass traditional, costly map providers like Tele Atlas, and to cover 140 countries more completely than professionally made maps.

한국어 해설:
구글의 관점: ① 지도 = 비즈니스의 전략적 자산 (특히 모바일 시대) ② 사용자가 직접 정확히 위치를 파악하는 것이 데이터 수집의 최선책 ③ 기존 지도 제공업체(Tele Atlas 등)를 우회(bypass)하여 비용 절감 + 더 완전한 커버리지 확보.
Question 4
What concern does Tele Atlas raise about community-created maps?
모범 답안 (영어):
Tele Atlas argues that its customers — who may be operating delivery trucks or emergency vehicles — cannot rely fully on community-created maps. Their customers require a level of due diligence and quality that far exceeds what a volunteer community can provide. Tele Atlas compares community-created maps to Wikipedia, suggesting that professionals cannot depend on them for critical, safety-related decisions, just as historians cannot rely on Wikipedia as a primary source.

한국어 해설:
텔레 아틀라스의 우려: 품질과 신뢰성(due diligence) 문제. 핵심 비유: 역사학자가 위키피디아에 의존할 수 없는 것처럼, 배달차량·긴급차량 운전자도 커뮤니티 지도에 전적으로 의존할 수 없다 → 전문적 검토와 높은 품질 기준이 필요.
Question 5
Why can community-edited maps become politically controversial?
모범 답안 (영어):
Community-edited maps can become politically controversial because maps reflect and reinforce political claims over territory. The article illustrates this with the Kashmir dispute: when Faraz Ahmad (Pakistani) tried to map the Pakistan-administered part of Kashmir, Map Maker initially rejected his contributions. Users from India also attempted to edit the same region to claim it for their country. Google ultimately had to block further changes to prevent "back-and-forth editing." This shows that when different nationalities contribute to the same map, territorial disputes are transferred into the digital mapmaking process.

한국어 해설:
지도의 정치적 성격: 지도는 영토 주권의 시각적 표현. 카슈미르 사례가 핵심 증거: ① 파키스탄 관할 지역 편집 → Map Maker 거부 ② 인도 사용자들의 반대 편집 시도 ③ 결국 구글이 해당 지역 변경 차단. 결론: 지리 정보에는 정치적 권력 관계가 반영되어 있어 커뮤니티 편집이 국가 간 분쟁을 촉발할 수 있다.

② used to be (과거 상태) — "a field that used to be the exclusive domain of professionals": 현재는 그렇지 않음을 암시

③ any more than ~ (비교 부정) — "can't rely on ~, any more than historians can rely on Wikipedia": "역사학자처럼 마찬가지로 ~도 할 수 없다"
