The balance between financial gain and cultural authenticity

The economic benefit of culturtainment makes it attractive to politicians and policy makers alike.

A potential increase in inbound visitor numbers [coupled with their demand for related goods and services (travel, accommodation, retail)] is an incentive for those within governments and authorities to work with cultural groups / in order to develop celebrations and commemorations into larger and more high profile events.

However, such commercialization risks culturtainment becoming homogeneous and losing its original 'message' that could lead to a dilution of audiences.

This could also lead to [smaller non-commercial independent events being set up that would only serve to divide audiences further].

This is something that planners and stakeholders will need to balance against potential financial gain.

Changing political, social and religious landscapes will lead to the emergence of new cultures, and with them new culturtainment experiences.

Overall this is a healthy growth sector of the entertainment industry, but one that by its very nature is delicate in the face of exploitation.

📖 개념 학습 및 실전 문제
➤ Culturtainment

The combination of culture and entertainment, where cultural elements such as history, traditions, or arts are presented in an engaging, entertaining, and often commercially appealing way to attract and involve audiences.

The term culturetainment (also spelled culturtainment) is a modern portmanteau of "culture" and "entertainment."

While it does not have a single credited "inventor" like a scientific discovery, its origins and rise can be traced through academic and industrial contexts starting in the late 1990s.

Q. Which of the following are examples of culturtainment? Select THREE correct answers.
① Gyeongbokgung Palace Night Tour transforms a historical palace into a nighttime attraction with performances and guided tours, allowing visitors to experience traditional culture in an entertaining way.
② Changdeokgung Palace limits large-scale entertainment events in order to preserve its historical authenticity and traditional atmosphere.
③ Squid Game is a global television series based on traditional games and social themes, leading to tourism, merchandise, and immersive experiences.
④ Boseong Green Tea Fields focuses mainly on agricultural production and scenic viewing, with limited development of entertainment-based cultural experiences.
⑤ Busan International Film Festival promotes cinema while also boosting tourism and strengthening the image of the host city.
정답 및 해설
정답: ①, ③, ⑤

②는 진정성 보존을 위해 오락적 요소를 '제한'하는 사례이므로 컬처테인먼트가 아니며, ④는 오락적 요소를 결합하지 않은 단순 농업 및 경관 위주의 장소이므로 정답에서 제외됩니다.

🏗️ 건축과 CAD 설계
스케치의 상실이 창의성에 미치는 영향

In 2012, the Yale School of Architecture held a symposium called "Is Drawing Dead?"

The bold title reflects a growing sense that the architect's freehand sketch is being displaced by the computer.

The transition from sketchpad to screen entails, many architects believe, a loss of creativity, of adventurousness.

Thanks to the precision and apparent completeness of screen renderings, a designer working at a computer has a tendency to lock in, visually and cognitively, on a design at an early stage.

He bypasses much of the reflective and exploratory playfulness that springs from the uncertainty and ambiguity of sketching.

Researchers term this phenomenon "premature fixation" and trace its cause to "the disincentive for design changes once a large amount of detail and interconnectedness is built too quickly into a CAD (Computer-Aided Design) model."

The designer at the computer also tends to emphasize formal experimentation at the expense of expressiveness.

By weakening an architect's "personal, emotional connection with the work," a renowned American architect and designer Michael Graves argues, CAD software produces designs that, "while complex and interesting in their own way," often "lack the emotional content of a design derived from hand."

📝 실전 문제 및 개념 학습
➤ 단어 의미 (Rendering)

In this passage, "rendering" means a detailed visual image of a design created by a computer to show what it will look like when finished.

For example:
- The architect showed a 3D rendering of the new building before construction started.
- This rendering helps clients imagine the final design clearly.

① avoid using digital tools in the early stages of the design process in order to maintain creativity
② tend to avoid design changes once a highly detailed and interconnected CAD model is formed too early
③ prefer maintaining ambiguity in early design stages rather than moving quickly toward clear and fixed forms
④ lack sufficient technical skills to effectively use CAD systems for complex architectural design

정답: ②
매우 상세하고 상호 연결된 CAD 모델이 너무 일찍 형성되면 디자인 변경을 피하는 경향이 있기 때문입니다.
