02 Passage — 원문

When gathering취합하다 the preferences of multiple agents행위자 into one collective choice, it is easily seen that certain cases .

For example, if there are two alternatives, a and b, and two agents such that one prefers a and the other one b, there is no deterministic결정론적인 way of selecting a single alternative without violating one of two basic fairness conditions known as anonymity익명성 and neutrality중립성.

Anonymity익명성 requires that the collective choice ought to be independent of the agents' identities whereas neutrality중립성 requires impartiality공정성 towards the alternatives.

Allowing lotteries as social outcomes hence seems like a necessity for impartial collective choice.

Indeed, most common "deterministic" social choice functions such as plurality rule are only deterministic as long as there is no tie동률, which is usually resolved by drawing a lot추첨하다.

The use of lotteries for the selection of officials interestingly goes back to the world's first democracy in Athens, where it was widely regarded as a principal characteristic of democracy, and has recently gained increasing attention in political science.

④ This process / means [that fossil-fuelled power plants and hazardous-waste facilities are disproportionately and deliberately situated in Black or ethnic-minority neighbourhoods].
④ 이 과정은 화석 연료 발전소와 유해 폐기물 시설이 흑인 또는 소수 민족 거주 지역에 불균형적으로 그리고 의도적으로 위치함을 의미한다. ★ means that ~: "~을 의미한다" / disproportionately and deliberately: 두 부사의 병렬 강조(불균형적 + 의도적)
⑤ As a result of this, / Black Americans / are three times more likely to die from exposure to air pollutants than their white counterparts.
⑤ 이로 인해, 흑인 미국인들은 대기 오염 물질에 노출되어 사망할 가능성이 백인 상대방보다 세 배 더 높다. ★ As a result of ~: "~의 결과로" / three times more likely: 배수 비교("세 배 더 ~할 가능성") / counterpart: "동등한 상대, 대응하는 사람"
🟢 핵심 구문 포인트

① What ~ was (관계대명사 what 주어절) — "What deeply affected ~ was not only ~ but also ~": What절이 주어, was = 동사, not only A but also B = 보어

② not only A but also B — "not only that ~ (metaphorically) but also that ~ (literally)": 두 that절이 병렬 — 은유적·문자적 이중 의미 강조

③ by which (관계사구) — "the process by which ~ is situated": = "the process in which ~ is situated by [someone]" — 전치사 전치 관계절

④ disproportionately and deliberately (부사 병렬) — 두 부사가 병렬로 "불균형적이고 의도적으로"를 강조 — 환경 인종차별의 구조적 의도성을 드러내는 핵심 표현

⑤ times more likely to V (배수 비교) — "three times more likely to die than ~": "~보다 V할 가능성이 N배 더 높다"

Main Idea

Black communities bear an unequal/unjust burden of environmental racism, as hazardous infrastructure is deliberately situated in their neighborhoods, leading to severe health consequences.
흑인 공동체는 환경적 인종차별의 불공평한 부담을 지고 있으며, 유해 인프라가 의도적으로 그들의 거주 지역에 위치하여 심각한 건강 결과로 이어진다.
