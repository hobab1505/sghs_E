03 Topic & Text Structure
Topic: The essential questions geography asks about spatial arrangements
지리학이 공간적 배치에 관해 묻는 핵심 질문들

Text Structure

Claim: Geography = asking "where," "why there," and "so what"
Support 1: Daily life (finding food, commuting) → spatial awareness required
Support 2: Larger scale (business/policy, events) → spatial awareness required
Support 3: Specific examples
→ New store location: population, roads, socio-economic data
→ Migration factors: territory, discrimination, physical environment

Main Idea (빈칸 완성)

Geography goes beyond simply asking "where" — it investigates how things occur in particular places and what spatial arrangements mean for decisions ranging from everyday activities to business, policy, and migration.
지리학은 단순히 "어디에"를 묻는 것을 넘어, 특정 장소에서 사물이 어떻게 발생하는지, 공간적 배치가 일상 활동부터 사업·정책·이주에 이르는 결정에 무엇을 의미하는지를 탐구한다.

도입 → (C): 도입에서 "우리의 감각이 우리를 속일 수 있다는 생각"을 제시 → (C)에서 "He realized that sometimes our senses give us a false impression" = 감각의 거짓 인상 구체적 예시(물 속 막대기). "He"가 도입의 Descartes를 받음.

(C) → (A): (C) 마지막: "everything we experience with our senses might be false" → (A)에서 이를 더 확장: "It might even be that we are asleep ... we can never know anything for sure." = 의심의 급진적 확장

(A) → (B): (A) 마지막: "we can never know anything for sure" → (B)에서 역접(But): "But Descartes then showed that there was one thing he could be sure of" = 반전: 확신할 수 있는 한 가지 → cogito ergo sum
02 구문 해석 — 완성된 지문 & 구문별 해석
[도입] The idea [that our senses may be deceiving속이다 us] was taken up(주제를) 다루다 by the French philosopher and mathematician René Descartes.
우리의 감각이 우리를 속이고 있을 수 있다는 생각은 프랑스의 철학자이자 수학자인 René Descartes에 의해 다루어졌다. ★ The idea that ~ (동격 that절) / be taken up: "다루어지다" (수동태)
[(C)-1] He realized [that sometimes our senses give us a false impression] — a straight stick in a glass of water appears to be bent구부러진, for example.
그는 때때로 우리의 감각이 우리에게 거짓 인상을 줄 수 있다는 것을 깨달았다 — 예를 들어 물 한 잔 속의 곧은 막대기가 구부러진 것처럼 보인다. ★ realized that ~: 목적어절 / appear to be bent: "구부러진 것처럼 보이다"
[(C)-2] And if we know that they can be unreliable, it would be wrong to trust them completely — everything [that we experience with our senses] might be false.

[(A)-1] It might even be [that we are asleep, and that the world we are experiencing is no more than a dream, or that God or some mischievous장난기 많은 power is making us believe things that are not really true].

[(A)-2] If that is the case, though, we can never know anything for sure.
그러나 만약 그것이 사실이라면, 우리는 어떤 것도 확실하게 알 수 없다. ★ If that is the case: "만약 그것이 사실이라면" / for sure: "확실히"
[(B)-1] But Descartes then showed [that there was one thing he could be sure of]: In order to be deceived속임을 당하다, he must exist, and exist as a thing that thinks.

[(B)-2] So, although his senses could not be trusted, he could be sure he existed as something [that was capable of thinking and reasoning].
따라서 비록 그의 감각은 신뢰할 수 없었지만, 그는 생각하고 추론할 수 있는 무언가로 존재한다는 것을 확신할 수 있었다. ★ although(양보절) / be capable of -ing: "~할 수 있는" → cogito ergo sum
