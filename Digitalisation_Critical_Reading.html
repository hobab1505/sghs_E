Reading Comprehension — 5 Questions

5 Q & A
Q1 — Continuity with tradition
Reading Comprehension
Why is digitalisation not simply a break from geographical tradition?
Model Answer
Geographers have long relied on technology — from the compass to GIS, from analogue mapping to artificial intelligence — to expand and deepen knowledge of the world. Digitalisation continues this tradition; it is "the latest chapter" rather than a rupture. The only difference is that it is unfolding at an unprecedented pace and scale.

핵심 단서: "digitalisation is not a rupture from geographical tradition but rather its latest chapter"
Q2 — Digital ↔ Physical inseparability
Reading Comprehension
What does the passage mean when it says that the digital and the physical are no longer separate?
Model Answer
Digital technologies are not free-floating; they depend on physical infrastructure — server farms, undersea cables, microchips — and operate within real social, political, and economic structures. So the old binary (virtual vs. real, online vs. offline) collapses: digital space is embedded in the physical world rather than floating above it.

핵심 단서: "The digital does not float free of the physical world; it is embedded in it."
Q3 — Distance & Proximity reconfigured
Reading Comprehension
How do digital platforms change people's experience of distance and proximity?
Model Answer
Platforms like dating apps, ridesharing, and short-term rentals turn what were once chance encounters into deliberate, choice-driven interactions. Messaging apps such as WhatsApp and WeChat allow people to maintain close relationships across vast distances with unprecedented ease. Distance is not eliminated but profoundly reconfigured — proximity becomes a matter of choice and platform design rather than physical location.

핵심 단서: "not eliminated, but profoundly reconfigured"
Q4 — Algorithmic Violence
Reading Comprehension
Why can digitalisation intensify spatial inequality?
Model Answer
The data used to train algorithmic systems carry the biases and inequalities of the societies that produced them. When such systems guide planning decisions, loan approvals, or housing allocations, they reproduce — and sometimes amplify — existing spatial disadvantage. Researchers call this algorithmic violence: harm caused through "ostensibly neutral computational processes." A more data-rich geography is therefore not automatically a more just one.

핵심 단서: "algorithmic violence: the reproduction, and sometimes amplification, of spatial disadvantage through ostensibly neutral computational processes"
Q5 — Dual-use technologies
Reading Comprehension
According to Maalsen, how can the same digital technologies serve opposite purposes?
Model Answer
The same tools (GeoAI, big-data platforms, satellite monitoring) can be used either for surveillance and extraction — tracking populations or exploiting resources — or for resistance and care: mapping environmental injustice, documenting displacement of vulnerable groups, and building digital networks that support marginalised communities. The technology itself is not the determining factor; whose hands it is in — and to what end — is what matters.

핵심 단서: "the same technologies that enable surveillance and extraction can also, in other hands, become tools of resistance and care"

🟢 핵심 구문 & 문법 종합 정리
A. 핵심 구조 패턴 (Core Structural Patterns)

① not A but rather B — "A가 아니라 오히려 B": ⑦ "not a rupture from tradition but rather its latest chapter" (글 전체의 핵심 명제 구조)

② not only A but also B — "A뿐만 아니라 B도": ㉖ "not only 'where?' but also 'for whom?' and 'at what cost?'"

③ not merely A. It is B — "단지 A가 아니라 B이다" (강조 분할): ㉝–㉞ "not merely studying ... It is helping to shape it"

④ A as well as B — "B뿐만 아니라 A도" (강조는 A): ⑮ "material conditions ... as well as ... social structures"

⑤ no less ~ than ~ — "~에 못지않게": ⑭ "no less real than so-called 'real' spaces"

B. 관계사 & 명사절 (Relative Clauses & Noun Clauses)

⑥ [what S+V] (관계대명사 what절) — "S가 V하는 것": ⑱ "what were once chance encounters" / ㉔ "what researchers have called algorithmic violence"

⑦ 전치사 + 관계대명사 — ⑮ "structures within which those technologies operate" / ⑩ "the ways in which knowledge is constructed"

⑧ [whose + 명사 + V] (간접의문문 / 관계사) — ㉜ "whose spatial knowledge counts": "누구의 공간 지식이 중요한지"

⑨ that 관계절 (제한적·계속적) — ⑨ "... infrastructures that challenge our ability" / ㉓ "societies that produced them"

C. 분사구문 & 분사구 (Participles)

⑩ 분사구문 병렬 (현재분사 3개) — ㉙ "mapping ~, documenting ~, or building ~": 결과·예시

⑪ 과거분사구 후치 수식 — ⑨ "For those interested in ~" / ㉓ "The data used to train algorithmic systems"

⑫ 형용사구 후치 수식 — ⑲ "with an ease unimaginable a generation ago"

D. 5형식 & 사역·재귀 (Causative & Reflexive)

⑬ transform A into B — ⑱ "A를 B로 변모시키다"

⑭ enable + O + to V — ⑲ "O가 V할 수 있게 하다"

⑮ prompt + O + to V — ㉖ "O가 V하도록 자극하다"

⑯ treat A as B / be understood to V — ⑫ "A를 B로 다루다" / ⑭ "~인 것으로 이해되다"

⑰ find oneself at ~ / V-ing — ㉚ "자신이 ~에 처해 있음을 발견하다" (주어 = 목적어 → 재귀대명사)

E. 기타 학습 포인트 (Other Notes)

⑱ 가정법 과거완료가 들어간 관계절 — ㉗ "capacities that would have seemed extraordinary even two decades ago": "20년 전이라면 ~으로 보였을"

⑲ 부분 부정 (not ~ automatically) — ㉕ "does not automatically produce a more just one": "자동적으로 ~한 것은 아니다"

⑳ 전치사 공유 구조 — ② "understanding of and engagement with space": 두 명사가 공통 목적어 space 공유

㉑ 대시(—)의 용법 — 강조 삽입(㉖ "the discipline's founding question") / 부연 예시(⑨ "cyberspace, the online, ...") / 강한 대조(㉑ "it can intensify it")

㉒ 세미콜론(;) 용법 — ⑯ "The digital does not float free of the physical world; it is embedded in it": 대조적 두 절 연결

Q1 — A connecting statement
Comparative Reading
Write a statement that best connects two articles.
Digital technologies have changed who can produce geographical knowledge and how space is understood, but not always equally or without consequence.
해설 — Why these words?
who — Online Maps에서 자원봉사 지도 제작자(volunteer cartographers), 위키 편집자, 일반 사용자가 지도 제작에 참여 가능 → 지식 생산의 주체(who)가 확대됨.
how — Maalsen이 강조한 "the questions we ask, how we ask and answer them"과 일치 → 공간을 이해하는 방식(how)이 바뀜.
equally — Maalsen의 algorithmic violence 논의와 Online Maps의 카슈미르 분쟁(편집권 불균형) 모두 "디지털 참여가 평등하지 않다"는 점을 보여줌.

대안 어휘: who → "the people who" / how → "the way" / equally → "evenly"
Q2 — Maalsen on volunteer cartography
Comparative Reading
What would Maalsen most likely say about volunteer cartography in Online Maps?
It shows how digital tools can democratize spatial knowledge, but it should be examined in terms of power, access, and inequality.
해설 — Why these words?
democratize — 자원봉사 지도 제작은 "tools of resistance and care" (Maalsen)의 대표 사례. 누구나 지도 제작에 참여 가능 → 공간 지식의 민주화.

대안 어휘: democratize → "broaden / expand / open up" / examined → "critically assessed / scrutinized / questioned / interrogated"
Q3 — Kashmir & embedded structures
Comparative Reading
The Kashmir dispute in Online Maps involves competing national claims over map edits. How does this support Maalsen's argument that digital spaces are shaped by "social, political, and economic structures"?
The Kashmir case shows that even a seemingly neutral technical platform (Google Map Maker) becomes a site of political contest — who can edit, whose version is accepted, and who holds final authority all reflect existing power structures. This is exactly what Maalsen means: digital space is not a neutral layer floating above the physical world but is embedded in the same social and political forces.
해설 — Why these words?
neutral (1회·2회) — Maalsen의 핵심 표현 "ostensibly neutral computational processes"의 정확한 echo. Google Map Maker는 표면적으로는 중립적 기술 도구지만, 실제로는 인도/파키스탄의 국가 주권 갈등이 그대로 옮겨오는 장.
power — "who can edit, whose version is accepted, and who holds final authority" → 모두 권력 관계를 묻는 질문. Maalsen이 "place is fundamental to how power operates"라고 명시.
embedded — Maalsen 본문의 핵심 어휘: "The digital does not float free of the physical world; it is embedded in it." 카슈미르 사례가 바로 이 명제의 실증.

핵심 통찰: 카슈미르 사례는 디지털 공간이 실제 정치 갈등의 연장임을 보여주는 가장 명확한 예시. "위에 떠 있는 가상 층(floating layer)"이라는 초기 디지털 담론은 더 이상 유효하지 않음.

대안 어휘: neutral → "objective / value-free / apolitical" / power → "political / authority / governance" / embedded → "rooted / grounded / entangled"
📌 Synthesis — Maalsen의 두 핵심 명제
Thesis 1: "The digital does not float free of the physical world; it is embedded in it."
디지털은 물리적 세계에서 분리되어 떠 있지 않으며, 그 안에 깊이 내재되어 있다.
Thesis 2: "A more precise and data-rich geography does not automatically produce a more just one."
더 정밀하고 데이터가 풍부한 지리학이 자동으로 더 정의로운 지리학을 만들어내는 것은 아니다.
🟢 비교 독해 핵심 — Maalsen의 입장

긍정: 디지털화는 지리학의 도구를 확장하고, 새로운 관계·만남을 가능하게 하며, 공간 지식의 생산자(who)를 확대한다.

비판: 그러나 그 과정은 결코 중립적이지 않다. 알고리즘은 사회의 편견을 재생산하고, 디지털 플랫폼은 기존 권력 구조 안에서 작동한다.

처방: "where?"뿐 아니라 "for whom?"과 "at what cost?"를 물어야 한다. 지리학은 디지털 세계를 단지 연구하는 것이 아니라 형성하는 데에 참여한다.
