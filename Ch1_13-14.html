03 Topic & Text Structure
Topic: The role of interoceptive body signals in the brain's perception of emotions through mimic of others
타인의 모방을 통한 감정 지각에서 내부 감각 신호의 역할

Text Structure

Introduction (sent. 1~2): 신체의 내부 감각 수용 기능의 역할 소개
→ 자신의 감정 조절 + 타인의 감정과의 접촉
Development 1 (sent. 3~5): 뇌의 한계 → 신체의 역할
→ 뇌는 타인 마음에 직접 접근 불가 / 신체가 conduit로서 본능적 정보 공급

→ 무의식적 mimic(표정·몸짓·음조) → 내부 감각 수용 → 타인 감정 지각

The body serves as a bridge for emotional empathy: by unconsciously mimicking others' expressions, we perceive their emotions through our own interoceptive signals — we are literally sampling their feelings.
신체는 감정적 공감의 다리 역할을 한다: 타인의 표정을 무의식적으로 모방함으로써, 우리는 자신의 내부 감각 신호를 통해 그들의 감정을 지각한다 — 우리는 문자 그대로 그들의 감정을 맛보고 있는 것이다.

McLuhan & the Overextension of Technology
기술의 과도한 확장과 맥루한 — 모든 확장은 잘라내기
빈칸 내용 추론
본문 — 원문

The strongest criticisms of the overextension과도한 확장 of technology involve claims of dehumanization비인간화, and these arguments are not new.

Canadian philosopher of the electronic age, Marshall McLuhan was primarily a techno-utopian기술 낙관론자, but he was also a critical thinker, who cautioned경고하다 decades ago against too much human extension into technology.

McLuhan famously claimed that every media extension of man is an amputation잘라내기, 절단.

For example, once we have a car, we don't walk to the shops any more; once we have a computer hard-drive, we don't have to remember things; and now that we have personal GPS (Global Positioning System) on our cell phones, no one can find their way about without it.

Based on McLuhan's philosophy, we are becoming post-literate탈문해화의 because we rely on screens full of pictures, rather than print media, such as books.

It is thus possible that extensions of human faculties기능들, 능력들, through techno- and bio-enhancement생물학적 향상, will bring about arrested정지된, 저해된 development in the natural evolution of higher human faculties.

* techno-utopian: 기술 낙관론자   ** amputation: 잘라내기, 절단   *** post-literate: 탈문해화의

Q. Fill-in-the-blank (보충 지문 — 빈칸 3개)
  Marshall McLuhan was a Canadian thinker who argued that media do not simply transmit information but actively (r) human experience. He believed that when humans extend their abilities through technology, they also reorganize how they perceive and interact with the world. A key idea is that every extension of human capacity tends to replace or weaken a (p) natural skill, which changes everyday behavior in unnoticed ways. For example, modern social media can extend communication across distance, but it may also reduce face-to-face attention and deep listening in conversations. This suggests that technology works like an environment that slowly reshapes (h) rather than just a tool we use. From this perspective, technological progress is not only about gaining new abilities but also about losing certain older forms of awareness. Overall, McLuhan's view helps explain why human development and technology should be understood as closely connected, sometimes in unexpected and hidden ways.
